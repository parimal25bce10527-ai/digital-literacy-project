Case Study: The "QR Code Receiving" UPI Scam

What the crime is: UPI fraud involves deceiving a victim into authorizing a financial transfer under false pretenses. A highly common variation targeting students is the "QR Code Receiving Scam."

How it typically happens: The scammer contacts a student who has listed an old textbook or PC gaming component for sale on an online marketplace. The scammer agrees to the price immediately without negotiation and claims they will send the money right away via a UPI app. However, instead of simply transferring the money to the student's phone number, the scammer sends a QR code or a "collect request" through the app. They falsely instruct the student to scan the code and enter their UPI PIN "to receive the payment." Because the student is distracted or rushing to close the sale, they follow the instructions.

Who is targeted: College students in India are frequent targets due to their high volume of peer-to-peer transactions, frequent use of online marketplaces, and sometimes limited awareness of how specific app mechanics work.

The consequences: The moment the student enters their UPI PIN, the funds are instantly deducted from their own bank account. Because UPI is a real-time settlement system, reversing the transaction is extremely difficult, resulting in immediate financial loss and significant stress for the student.S
