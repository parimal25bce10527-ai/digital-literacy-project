Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
5 Do's:

Do audit your privacy settings: Regularly check who can view your posts and personal information on platforms like Instagram and Facebook.

Do curate a professional presence: Use platforms like LinkedIn to highlight your coding projects, college activities, and tech skills.

Do verify before sharing: Always fact-check news or campus announcements before forwarding them to batch groups to prevent misinformation.

Do pause before posting: Assume everything you post is permanent; take a second to ensure it reflects well on your personal brand.

Do engage respectfully: Disagree politely in comment sections and academic forums without resorting to personal attacks.

5 Don'ts:

Don't overshare personal details: Never post photos containing your college ID card, home address, or travel tickets with visible barcodes.

Don't engage in cyberbullying: Avoid participating in online harassment, trolling, or spreading rumors about peers.

Don't ignore your digital footprint: Remember that future internship coordinators and employers will likely Google your name.

Don't mix professional and personal accounts: Keep your GitHub and LinkedIn strictly professional; save the casual posts for private Instagram accounts.

Don't plagiarize content: Always give proper credit when sharing someone else's artwork, code snippets, or photography online.
